# Resume Note

## Last Session

- We bootstrapped the first `Skills-Gaming` learning system around market landscape, company models, role-based capability building, core systems, and an AI-assisted hackathon playbook.

## What I Understand Now

- `Skillz` is structurally different from `Papaya` and `AviaGames`: one is much closer to a developer platform, while the others are closer to first-party content portfolios.
- In this category, “fairness” is not a nice-to-have; it is part of the product itself.
- For your background, the key transition is not from “platform” to “game engine code”, but from “building reusable systems” to “building reusable systems that protect fun, fairness, and operational clarity”.
- AI can compress ideation, art iteration, copy, QA, and production coordination, but it cannot replace judgment on game feel and scope control.

## What Still Feels Fuzzy

- We now have a first concept pool, but still need to narrow it to one or two directions with stronger confidence.
- We still need a more explicit view of compliance/platform constraints by region.
- We have a first retention / LiveOps layer, but still need deeper economy and compliance edges.
